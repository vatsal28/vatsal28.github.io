A bit about me

Welcome to my corner of the internet. This isn't a polished portfolio or a carefully curated LinkedIn presence. Think of it more like a garden: things grow here, some ideas bloom, others get pruned.

By day, I'm a Senior Engineering Analyst at Google, working on production ML and AI systems. I spend my time thinking about analytics, data pipelines, LLM governance, and making sure things don't break when they go live. It's the kind of work where you celebrate when nothing happens, because nothing happening means everything is working.

By night (and weekends), I tinker. I've got a homelab running on a Raspberry Pi and a mini-PC: Pi-hole blocks the ads, WireGuard lets me tunnel home from anywhere, Nextcloud keeps my files synced, and Plex serves up movies to every screen in the house. There's also a 4070 Ti Super rig for running local AI experiments with Ollama.

The common thread? I like building systems that work quietly in the background, making life a little easier without demanding attention.

FakeOut

This one started from a simple frustration: it's genuinely hard to know what's real online anymore. AI-generated images are everywhere, and misinformation spreads faster than corrections.

FakeOut is my answer. One tap gives you fact-checks from trusted sources and AI image detection. No PhD required. It started as an Android app, built mostly with AI-assisted coding (I'm not a mobile developer by trade), and has grown to 11,000+ downloads with 4.2+ stars on Google Play. Recently launched on iOS too.

The app has been featured in multiple YouTube videos. Turns out people really want tools to navigate information chaos.

Meal planning is one of those problems that seems simple until you actually try to solve it. MealMate was my attempt: an AI-powered meal planning assistant that considers what you have, what you like, and what's realistic for a Tuesday night when you're tired.
